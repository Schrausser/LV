Android exposure calculator Lightvalues LV for calculating aperture values Av corresponding to light values1 by shifting the time value Tv=s-1 or arithmetic ISO speed value S in steps k according to the common classification, where

Tvn−k=Tvn·2k,Sn+k=Sn·2k,(1)

Tvn+k=Tvn2k,Sn−k=Sn2k(2)

and

Av(Tvn)=Av(Tvn−k)·2k=Av(Tvn+k)2k,(3)

Av(Sn)=Av(Sn+k)·2k=Av(Sn−k)2k.(4)

Therefore Av is calculated from Tv or S by

AvTv=AvTv0·aTv,AvS=AvS0·aS(5)

with

aTv=212·log⁡2Tv0Tv=e12·log⁡Tv0Tv,(6)

aS=212·log⁡2SS0=e12·log⁡SS0.(7)

The shutter speed is set in the range between Tv=32000 and 2 hours Tv=0.000138_ , aperture ranges from Av=0.5 to Av=152 and speed S is set to range between ISO 0.4 and ISO 102400. On aperture, shutter speed and exposure see e.g. Roberts (1995), Beaver (2018), Bernacki (2020) and Simon et al. (2022).

Logarithmic speed S° (s. Allbright, 1991) is transformed from arithmetic speed S by

S°=10·log⁡10(S)+1=10·log⁡(S)log⁡(10)+1,(8)

S=10S°−110.(9)

The exposure value Ev is calculated from Tv and Av , where

Ev=log⁡2Av2Tv−1=log⁡(Tv·Av2)log⁡(2),Tv=2EvAv2,Av=2Ev·TvTv.(10)

The total luminous flux or illuminance EV in lux lx , where lx=lmm2 results from Ev and S by

EV=250·2EvS.(11)

For logarithmic functions in general see e.g. Marsden and Weinstein (1985), Howie (2001) and Sobot (2021).

Presets for time and aperture combinations at ISO 100/21° (8) are given (s. Tab. 1), with aperture values Av ​​are rounded to one decimal place. Custom time-aperture-ISO combinations for exposure values Ev (10) or illuminance EV (11) can be achieved by shifting Av itself (c.f. Fig. 1).
